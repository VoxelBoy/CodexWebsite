Designing Sandbox City for Millions of Creative Players

Sandbox City started as a passion project: a place where creators could drop in, sketch a game mechanic, and immediately share it with friends. Scaling that idea to millions of players required a deliberate approach to system design, a relentless focus on iteration speed, and an obsession with giving players trustworthy tools.

Designing for safe experimentation

Sandbox City is driven by real-time simulation systems—physics, AI, economy, and social spaces—that players can bend without breaking. We built our runtime sandboxing layer on top of Unity’s Entity Component System (ECS) so creators can author logic with high-level nodes that compile down to Burst-compiled jobs. Any experiment executes in an isolated world stream, and the engine only merges state back into the shared shard after validation checks succeed.

This approach lets us ship new gameplay building blocks weekly. Designers define constraints as C# attributes in our authoring toolkit, while creators drag-and-drop behaviours that are guaranteed to perform. The combination keeps user-generated content safe and performant without slowing players down.

Scaling tooling beyond the core team

Internal tooling had to be just as polished as the player-facing UI. We invested early in a toolkit guild: a small group inside Sandbox City that maintains our scripting language, metrics dashboards, and release automation. Their mandate is to ship user-facing features only when the supporting tools are ready. That discipline means even our live ops team can spin up seasonal events without engineering help.

  • Prefab recipes generate best-practice setups for creators and reduce the risk of misconfigured physics scenes.
  • Scenario replays capture Burst job traces so we can reproduce creator bugs in seconds.
  • Zero-downtime publishing keeps community hubs live while updates stream in.

Lessons for other live UGC experiences

Working on Sandbox City has reinforced a few principles that apply to any live UGC platform:

  1. Guardrails beat gatekeeping. Expose powerful systems, but bake in constraints so creators can move fast without risking the platform.
  2. Invest in observability. Our creator analytics pipeline feeds directly into design decisions; without it, we’d be guessing where to allocate engineering time.
  3. Celebrate co-creation. Weekly community showcases and in-engine tutorials sustain the creativity loop.

Sandbox City is still evolving, but the foundations—ECS-powered tooling, disciplined live operations, and a focus on empowering creators—will keep it resilient. If you’re building large-scale UGC experiences in Unity, I’d love to compare notes.
